# Bitcoin (BTC) Mining Guide

> **Algorithm:** SHA-256 | **Current Price:** ~$97,000 | **[Price History](price-history/btc.csv)**

## Overview

Bitcoin is the world's first and largest cryptocurrency by market cap, secured by the SHA-256 proof-of-work algorithm. It remains the most competitive coin to mine, attracting industrial-scale operations worldwide. Despite intense competition, BTC mining is still profitable for operators with access to cheap electricity and modern ASIC hardware.

## Is BTC Worth Mining Right Now?

Bitcoin mining profitability is highly sensitive to BTC price and electricity cost. At $0.06/kWh, modern ASICs like the S21 XP remain profitable around the $60,000–$70,000 price range. With BTC above $90,000 (as of early 2026), top-tier hardware earns strong margins. Miners paying over $0.10/kWh will find margins tight on older hardware. The next halving in 2028 will cut rewards to 1.5625 BTC, making efficiency the key long-term differentiator.
