# Pick Your First Miner (Under $3,000)

*How to read the numbers, set a budget, and choose hardware you won't regret.*

---

## Before You Buy Anything: Understand the Numbers

Most beginners look at a miner's price and hashrate, then assume the math works out. It often doesn't — at least not without checking a few key figures first.

### The Three Numbers That Matter

**1. Daily revenue** — How much the miner earns per day in cryptocurrency, at current coin prices and network difficulty.

**2. Daily electricity cost** — How much power the miner draws, multiplied by your electricity rate. A 3,500W miner running 24/7 uses 84 kWh per day. At $0.10/kWh, that's $8.40/day.

**3. Daily profit** — Revenue minus electricity cost. This is what actually goes in your pocket.

Use [WhatToMine](https://whattomine.com) or [ASIC Miner Value](https://www.asicminervalue.com) to get current estimates. Plug in your electricity rate — the default is rarely what you pay.

---

## What Is ROI (Return on Investment)?

**ROI** tells you how long it takes for your miner to pay back what you spent on it.

### Simple ROI Formula

```
ROI (days) = Miner Cost ÷ Daily Profit
```

**Example:**
- Miner cost: $1,200
- Daily revenue: $6.50
- Daily electricity cost: $2.00 (at $0.10/kWh)
- Daily profit: $4.50
- ROI: $1,200 ÷ $4.50 = **267 days (~9 months)**

Is 9 months good? It depends. Many miners target 12–18 month ROI. Under 12 months is considered strong. Over 24 months is risky — coin prices and difficulty can shift significantly over two years.

### What ROI Does NOT Account For

- Coin price changes (up or down)
- Network difficulty increases (more miners join → each earns less)
- Hardware failure
- Resale value of the miner after you're done

Always treat ROI estimates as a starting point, not a guarantee.

## The Importance of Electricity Cost

Here's the same miner at different electricity rates:

| Electricity Rate | Daily Cost | Daily Profit | ROI |
|-----------------|------------|--------------|-----|
| $0.05/kWh | $1.00 | $5.50 | ~218 days |
| $0.10/kWh | $2.00 | $4.50 | ~267 days |
| $0.15/kWh | $3.00 | $3.50 | ~343 days |
| $0.20/kWh | $4.00 | $2.50 | ~480 days |

*Based on example miner earning $6.50/day, costing $1,200.*

If your electricity rate is above $0.15/kWh, many miners become marginal or unprofitable. Some miners host their equipment in data centers with cheap power — called **colocation** or **co-lo hosting** — to solve this problem.

## New vs. Used Miners

### Buying New

- Full manufacturer warranty (usually 180 days)
- Latest firmware and efficiency
- Higher upfront cost
- Easier to get support

### Buying Used

- Significantly lower cost — sometimes 30–50% off
- No warranty or expired warranty
- Unknown wear history — could have issues
- Good deals exist, but due diligence is essential

**Tips for buying used:**
- Ask for a video of the miner running (showing hashrate and temperature on the dashboard)
- Check the fan noise for anything unusual (grinding or rattling indicates bearing wear)
- Avoid sellers who won't provide proof of operation
- Factor in potential repair costs when calculating ROI

## Final Checklist Before You Buy

- [ ] Do I know my electricity rate ($/kWh)?
- [ ] Have I calculated daily profit and ROI at current coin prices?
- [ ] Do I have a place to put the miner (good ventilation, noise tolerance)?
- [ ] Do I have the right power outlet/circuit for the wattage?
- [ ] Am I buying from a reputable seller?
- [ ] Have I accepted that prices and difficulty will change?
